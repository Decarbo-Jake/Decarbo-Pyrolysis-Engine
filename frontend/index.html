Feedstock & operating point
Feed rate (kg/h ar)
Primary throughput. Thermal limit is where Q_required (rising with feed) equals Q_delivered (fixed by reactor geometry).
2000
Moisture (% ar)
Moisture dilutes LHV and increases heat demand. High moisture reduces dry feed rate and CO₂ sequestration.
10.0
Pyrolysis temp (°C)
Higher temp increases biochar quality (lower H/C) but increases Q_required, reducing max feed rate. Typical: 450-700°C.
550
Operating hours/yr
Affects annual CO₂ totals only. Typical with maintenance: 7500-8200 h/yr.
8000
Biochar Corg (% dry)
Organic carbon from lab. Drives CO₂ sequestration calc. EBC Basic requires ≥50% Corg. Jibito S01: 54.1%.
54.1
Biochar H/C molar
H/C <0.4: 95% permanence (EBC Premium). H/C 0.4-0.7: 90% (EBC Basic). H/C ≥0.7: not stable biochar.
0.26
Rotary kiln — geometry & materials
Drum diameter (m)
HX area = π × D × L. Larger diameter raises the thermal limit proportionally.
2.40
Heated length (m)
Doubling length doubles Q_delivered and max feed rate. Most effective lever for increasing throughput.
6.0
Wall thickness (mm)
R_wall = t/λ. For SS304 12mm: R_wall = 0.00075 m²K/W — only ~9% of total resistance. Pyrolysis-side HTC dominates.
12
Combustion gas T (°C)
Temperature OUTSIDE the drum (not the PLC reading). ΔT = T_comb − T_pyr drives Q_delivered = U × A × ΔT.
900
h conv outside (W/m²K)
Convective HTC combustion gas side. At >700°C radiation (250-350 W/m²K) dominates and this term is secondary.
50
h conv inside (W/m²K)
Convection only inside drum. Internal radiation (~150-200 W/m²K) added automatically by the engine.
35
Shell steel grade
Sets max service temperature. Combustion gas above this limit causes creep deformation over time.

Combustion chambers
Dual-chamber configuration (RBu + PCC)
RBu — reactor burner
Internal diameter (mm)
Cylindrical RBu internal diameter after refractory. Jibito: 1300mm. Volume = π × (D/2)² × L.
1300
Internal length (mm)
RBu internal length. Jibito: 2800mm. With D=1300mm gives V=3.72 m³.
2800
Syngas split to RBu (%)
Fraction of total syngas directed to RBu. More to RBu = more kiln heat, longer PCC τ. Less to RBu = better EU WID margin in PCC.
40
Excess air RBu (%)
Excess combustion air above stoichiometric. Higher ensures complete combustion but increases volumetric flow. Typical: 20-40%.
30
PCC — post-combustion chamber (EU WID)
Internal width (mm)
PCC internal width. Volume = W × H × L. EU WID requires τ ≥ 2.0s at ≥850°C. Jibito: 2000×4000×4000mm = 32 m³.
2000
Internal height (mm)
PCC internal height. EU WID is a legal requirement — must hold ≥850°C for ≥2.0s continuously.
4000
Internal length (mm)
Longer PCC directly increases τ and EU WID compliance margin.
4000
Excess air PCC (%)
Excess air in PCC. Lower than RBu typical (20-30%). Higher increases flue gas volume and reduces τ.
20
